# Issues

## Problems

- "Loot cave" exploits reported during the betas are still in the game.
- Solo players tend to lose out on Corrupted Breach participation rewards to groups. Wild guess as to why: Solo players are each counted as their own groups, but group damage or hit tags are always greater in actual groups, so solo players are unlikely to get credit for participation unless they can outdamage actual groups.
- Healers will lose out on Corrupted Breach participation rewards simply because the Life Staff and healing abilities do not provide enough damage output.
- During wars, cosmetic skins should be disabled. Skins can be used to conceal equipment from players.
- During wars, respawning on contested points should be disallowed.
- During wars, and before the gates open, trading items that are not unique to the war instance should be allowed.
- Servers should not undergo noncritical maintenance during peak hours and noncritical updates should be staggered based on war schedules.
- When sorting items by weight in your inventory or storage, weights are displayed incorrectly.
- Some apparel items have been assigned the wrong weight class. For example, a Harvester shirt is classed as Medium Chestwear but with a weight of 3.5 and Light resistances.
- The Light, Medium, and Heavy Filters in the Trading Post correspond to weight, instead of class.
- Servers can fast forward 20 days into the future, triggering all wars, invasions, and darkness events. If affected servers are rolled back, and character data is not, all affected players will have 20 days added to their `/played` time and receive the Time Played III achievement ("Play for 320 hours") and progress toward the Time Played IV achievement.
- The 1.0.2 patch introduced this fix: "Fixed an issue reducing variety and potential quantity of loot obtained from game modes and chests within the world." This fix flooded the market with refining materials, destroying the economy and trivializing crafting.
- During an invasion, only members of the company whose territory is under siege cannot use crafting stations in that territory. Other players are not subject to the same restrictions.
- During an invasion, players cannot enter an imaginary region around the subject fort. A message is displayed: "Restricted area, region is blocked." This message is confusing to players unfamiliar with invasion mechanics.
- The company treasury counter can get in a state where the counter is frozen until the player logs out and in again.

## Features

- The game needs raid groups: limiting groups to 5 members is unnecessarily restrictive and actively works against team dynamics. Friends want to play with each other. Companies want to play with each other.
- There is much to gather and craft, but there are only 4 ways to sort items in predefined item type categories. Players need to be able to create their own named "tabs" (categories) where they can drag-and-drop specific items.
- Players should be able to tag items with custom names so they can remember why they locked a crafted item in a sea of items, or so those items can be set up to be automatically sent to specific tabs.
- Players should be able to block certain items from being looted. For example, if a player doesn't care about Fishing, s/he may not want to collect fishing materials when harvesting Bulrush to make potions.
- When a player has zero coins, the game is nearly unplayable. That player cannot post sell trades, there's no trade channel for manually advertising trades (players can't paste into chat, so even if there was a trade channel, it would be tedious), players can't repair, players can't buy faction gear, and players can't craft due to taxes. Players can kill mobs and complete town board quests, but both offer very little income. It's strange that having no money locks players out of the economy.
- Trading Post search should support filtering results by attributes.
- You should be able to find items in your inventory or storage by any keyword in their tooltips, not only by their names.
- When the game is restarted, the active chat channel should default to the last selected.
- In the Trading Post, the perk browser window should display only valid perks for the active tab. For example, weapon perks do not appear on apparel items, so when the Apparel tab is the active tab, weapon perks should not be displayed in the perk browser window.
- When players sign up for invasions and wars, they should have to indicate their role: healer, damage, or tank.
- Leaders should be able to assign invasion and war groups to specific roles.
